Engineering Ethics Question 1:

Who developed virtue ethics?

  1. Jeremy Bentham
  2. Immanuel Kant
  3. Aristotle
  4. John Stuart Mill

Answer (Detailed Solution Below)

Option 3 : Aristotle

Engineering Ethics Question 1 Detailed Solution

The correct answer is:

c) Aristotle

Key Points Virtue ethics was developed by the ancient Greek philosopher Aristotle. He extensively explored the concept of virtues and character in his works, particularly in his ethical writings such as the "Nicomachean Ethics." Aristotle's virtue ethics focuses on cultivating virtuous character traits and achieving eudaimonia, often translated as "flourishing" or "living well," through the development of moral virtues and practical wisdom.

This ethical framework emphasizes the importance of cultivating good habits and virtuous behavior to lead a morally fulfilling life.

Additional Information Virtue ethics is a philosophical approach to ethics that emphasizes the development of moral character and virtues as the foundation for ethical behavior. It is concerned with answering questions about how individuals should live their lives, what constitutes a good life, and how one should make moral decisions. Virtue ethics places less emphasis on rules or consequences and more on the qualities of character and the cultivation of virtues.

Engineering Ethics Question 2:

Which of the following integrations is related to supernational organizational structure?

  1. Free Trade Area
  2. Economic union
  3. Common Market
  4. Economic Integration

Answer (Detailed Solution Below)

Option 4 : Economic Integration

Engineering Ethics Question 2 Detailed Solution

The correct answer is Economic Integration.

Key Points Economic integration refers to the process of reducing trade barriers and fostering economic cooperation among multiple countries. It involves the integration of economies to promote the free flow of goods, services, capital, and labor. Economic integration can take various forms, including:

Free trade agreements (FTAs): These agreements aim to reduce or eliminate tariffs and non-tariff barriers on trade between participating countries. Examples include the North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA) and the Comprehensive and Progressive Agreement for Trans-Pacific Partnership (CPTPP).

Customs unions: In a customs union, member countries eliminate trade barriers among themselves and establish a common external tariff on goods imported from non-member countries. The Southern Common Market (Mercosur) is an example of a customs union.

Common markets: A common market goes beyond a customs union by also allowing for the free movement of factors of production, such as capital and labor, in addition to goods and services. The European Economic Community (EEC) and the European Union's Single Market are examples of common markets.

Additional Information

Free Trade Agreement (FTA): An FTA is a form of economic integration that aims to reduce or eliminate trade barriers, such as tariffs and quotas, between participating countries. FTAs promote trade liberalization and provide preferential treatment to member countries, encouraging increased economic cooperation and market access.

Common Market: A common market goes beyond an FTA by not only eliminating trade barriers but also allowing for the free movement of goods, services, capital, and labor among member countries. In addition to facilitating trade, a common market aims to create a level playing field for businesses and promote economic integration by harmonizing regulations and standards.

Economic Union: An economic union represents a higher level of economic integration than a common market. In addition to the free movement of goods, services, capital, and labor, an economic union typically involves deeper coordination of economic policies, including monetary policy, fiscal policy, and regulatory frameworks. It may also include the establishment of a common currency and a central bank.

Engineering Ethics Question 3:

Assertion (A): Change made in strategy is unrelated to an organization’s structure

Reason (R): Structure of the organization majorly dictates how objectives and policies are going to be established

  1. Both (A) and (R) are correct, and (R) is the correct explanation of (A).
  2. Both (A) and (R) are correct, but (R) is not the correct explanation of (A).
  3. (A) is correct, but (R) is not correct.
  4. (A) is wrong, and (R) is correct.

Answer (Detailed Solution Below)

Option 4 : (A) is wrong, and (R) is correct.

Engineering Ethics Question 3 Detailed Solution

The correct answer is (A) is wrong, and (R) is correct.

Key Points

  • A company's strategy can cause the structure to change or shift.
  • As a business creates specific goals, marketing strategies, and market analysis, it may need to change its structure.
  • For example, a company whose goal is to open a new location may need to adjust its teams and department sizes.
  • They may choose to revise their structure to accomplish the new goals from their business strategy.
  • So, Change made in strategy is definitely related to an organization’s structure.
  • Thus Assertion A is wrong.
  • An organization's structure can help the company reach its business goals.
  • As the business grows and operates, the structure may change as leadership adds new positions and employees.
  • This change to the structure may eventually change the strategy.
  • Thus Reason R is correct.

Additional InformationStrategy:

  • Strategy is the action steps a business takes to reach its goals.
  • This is typically part of the business plan, which is a document detailing a business's goals and approaches.
  • A business strategy can detail many company elements, including Marketing plans, Branding ideas, Price models, Growth plans, and Market analysis.

Structure:

  • An organization's structure reflects the overall company formation.
  • The organizational structure describes many components within the business, including People in the company, Leadership positions and teams, Job positions and the number of employees, Technical procedures, Business methods, etc.

Hence, the correct answer is (A) is wrong, and (R) is correct.

What is meant by 'Conflict of Interest'?

  1. Being interested in many subjects
  2. Hobbies interfering in education
  3. Least interest in the job taken up or assigned
  4. A conflict between the private interests and the official responsibilities of a person in a position of trust

Answer (Detailed Solution Below)

Option 4 : A conflict between the private interests and the official responsibilities of a person in a position of trust

Engineering Ethics Question 6 Detailed Solution

Download Solution PDF

Conflict of interest:

  • It is an opposition between the private interests and the official or professional responsibilities of a person in a position of trust, power, and/or authority.
  • Such conflicts may result in a situation in which personal, financial, and/or nonfinancial considerations have the potential to influence or compromise professional judgment in service, research, consultation, instruction, administration, or any other professional activity.
  • It is important to note that it is not necessary for such influence or compromise to have occurred before a situation can be identified as a conflict of interest.
  • It is sufficient for the situation to appear to provide the potential for professional judgment to be compromised.

‘Utilitarianism’ in the professional ethics is

  1. an acquired habit that helps to lead a rational life
  2. a skill to solve a current ethical problem by comparing it with similar problems from the past and their outcome
  3. a right of activists to decide their own duties
  4. a judgment of an action by the consequences of that action

Answer (Detailed Solution Below)

Option 4 : a judgment of an action by the consequences of that action

Engineering Ethics Question 7 Detailed Solution

Download Solution PDF

Key Points

Utilitarianism:

  • It is an ethical theory that determines right from wrong by focusing on outcomes. It is a form of consequentialism. So, it is a judgment of an action by the consequences of that action.
  • Utilitarianism holds that the most ethical choice is the one that will produce the greatest good for the greatest number.
  • It is also the most common approach to moral reasoning used in business because of the way in which it accounts for costs and benefits.

Important Points

As we cannot predict the future, it is difficult to know with certainty whether the consequences of our actions will be good or bad. This is one of the limitations of utilitarianism.

  1. Professional offense

Option 4 : Professional malpractice
